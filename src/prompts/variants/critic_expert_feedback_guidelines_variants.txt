# Critic Expert Feedback Guidelines Variants

## Variant 1: Basic Feedback Guidelines
When you approve the expert's answer and reasoning trace, you must provide feedback on why you approve of the answer and reasoning trace. When you reject the answer and/or reasoning trace, you must provide feedback on how to improve the answer and/or reasoning trace. Structure the feedback as if you were having a conversation with the expert. Tell the expert what you approve of, what you do not approve of, and how to improve the answer and reasoning trace.

## Variant 2: Comprehensive Feedback Guidelines
Provide comprehensive feedback that addresses all aspects of the expert's answer. When approving, explain why the answer is effective and complete. When rejecting, provide detailed guidance on how to improve the answer, including specific suggestions for reasoning and analysis. Structure feedback as a constructive conversation with the expert.

## Variant 3: Strategic Feedback Guidelines
Provide strategic feedback that focuses on the expert's answer effectiveness and approach. When approving, highlight the strategic strengths of the answer. When rejecting, provide strategic guidance on how to improve the analytical approach and effectiveness. Structure feedback as strategic guidance for the expert.

## Variant 4: Systematic Feedback Guidelines
Provide systematic feedback that addresses each aspect of the expert's answer. When approving, explain how each component contributes to the answer's effectiveness. When rejecting, provide systematic guidance on how to improve each aspect. Structure feedback as systematic guidance for the expert.

## Variant 5: Targeted Feedback Guidelines
Provide targeted feedback that focuses on the most important aspects of the expert's answer. When approving, highlight the key strengths that make the answer effective. When rejecting, provide focused guidance on the most critical improvements needed. Structure feedback as targeted guidance for the expert.
