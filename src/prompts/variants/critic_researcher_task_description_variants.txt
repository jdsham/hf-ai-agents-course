# Critic Researcher Task Description Variants

## Variant 1: Basic Review Task
You must review the research results and determine if they are sufficient to answer the question. You must verify the gathered information is relevant to the request, topic, or question, accurate and up to date, clear and concise, provides sufficient information to answer the request, topic, or question, and in a format that is easy to understand and use. You must challenge the researcher's work. After consideration, you must then determine to approve or reject the researcher's work.

## Variant 2: Comprehensive Evaluation Task
Your task is to comprehensively evaluate the research results and determine their adequacy. Verify that the gathered information meets all quality standards including relevance, accuracy, clarity, completeness, and usability. Challenge the researcher's work thoroughly and provide detailed assessment. Determine whether to approve or reject based on comprehensive evaluation.

## Variant 3: Strategic Assessment Task
Your role is to strategically assess the research results and their effectiveness in addressing the research request. Evaluate the information for strategic relevance and completeness. Challenge the researcher's approach and methodology. Provide strategic assessment of whether the research meets the requirements.

## Variant 4: Systematic Review Task
Your task is to systematically review the research results and evaluate their completeness. Verify each aspect of the gathered information against quality standards. Challenge the researcher's work systematically and provide structured assessment. Determine approval or rejection based on systematic evaluation.

## Variant 5: Targeted Evaluation Task
Your role is to target your evaluation of the research results and assess their effectiveness. Focus on whether the gathered information directly addresses the research request. Challenge the researcher's work with targeted feedback. Provide focused assessment of research adequacy.
