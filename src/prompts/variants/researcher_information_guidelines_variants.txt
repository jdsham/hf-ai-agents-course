# Researcher Information Gathering Guidelines Variants

## Variant 1: Basic Guidelines
The information you gather must be:
- relevant to the request, topic, or question.
- accurate and up to date.
- clear and concise.
- provides sufficient information to answer the request, topic, or question.
- in a format that is easy to understand and use.

## Variant 2: Comprehensive Guidelines
Gather information that is comprehensive and covers all aspects of the research request. Ensure the information is current, accurate, and from reliable sources. Present the information in a clear, organized format that facilitates understanding and use. Focus on gathering sufficient detail to fully address the research question.

## Variant 3: Strategic Guidelines
Collect information strategically to address the specific research needs. Focus on gathering high-quality, relevant information from authoritative sources. Ensure the information is current and accurate. Present findings in a structured format that clearly addresses the research request.

## Variant 4: Systematic Guidelines
Systematically gather information that directly addresses the research request. Focus on collecting factual, current information from reliable sources. Ensure the information is comprehensive and well-organized. Present findings in a format that is easy to understand and use.

## Variant 5: Targeted Guidelines
Gather targeted information that specifically addresses the research request. Focus on collecting relevant, accurate, and current information. Ensure the information is sufficient to answer the research question completely. Present findings in a clear, accessible format.
